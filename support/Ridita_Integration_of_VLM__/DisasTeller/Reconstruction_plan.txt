# Wajima Region Reconstruction Plan Report: Earthquake Recovery and Resilience Strategy

## 1. Executive Summary
The Wajima region recently experienced a significant seismic event, resulting in a complex and heterogeneous damage distribution across the surveyed sectors. This report presents a comprehensive Reconstruction Plan, designed to facilitate a transition from emergency response to sustainable long-term recovery.

The immediate post-disaster phase was characterized by the establishment of critical medical and operational hubs in stable G1 zones (Wajima Drama Memorial Hall and Central Nishikigawa Street). The primary challenges identified include the total collapse of critical transport infrastructure (Concrete Bridge, G5) and severe structural devastation in residential and commercial districts (South Central Asaichi Street, G5).

This reconstruction plan outlines a phased approach:
*   **Phase I (Immediate, 0–3 months):** Stabilization, humanitarian relief, and temporary infrastructure restoration (e.g., temporary bridging).
*   **Phase II (Mid-term, 3–18 months):** Structural rehabilitation, debris clearance, and socioeconomic revitalization.
*   **Phase III (Long-term, 18+ months):** Resilient urban planning, permanent infrastructure development, and policy reform for disaster risk reduction.

We estimate a comprehensive budget of $850 Million to $1.2 Billion for full restoration, dependent on detailed site surveying. This report provides the framework for government funding allocation and inter-agency coordination.

## 2. Assessment of Damage
The assessment, derived from high-resolution structural analysis, categorizes the six key surveyed locations by disaster severity:

*   **G1 (Minimal/No Damage):**
    *   **Wajima Drama Memorial Hall:** Structurally sound; currently serving as the Command and Primary Medical Triage Center.
    *   **Central Nishikigawa Street:** Stable; utilized as the hub for temporary shelter and logistics staging.
*   **G3 (Moderate Structural Damage):**
    *   **North Asaichi Street:** Significant facade fracturing and structural compromise of stone-masonry buildings.
*   **G4 (Severe Structural Damage):**
    *   **Hama Street:** Catastrophic failure of structural corners, blocking primary transit arteries.
*   **G5 (Total Collapse/Uninhabitable):**
    *   **Concrete Bridge:** Complete span failure; critical logistical bottleneck.
    *   **South Central Asaichi Street:** Total loss of high-rise structures; extensive debris; ground currently impassable.

## 3. Reconstruction Needs
Reconstruction requires a blend of capital investment, specialized labor, and raw materials. Drawing on historical data from similar seismic events (e.g., the 2011 Tohoku reconstruction model), the following resource needs have been calculated:

### 3.1 Financial Requirements (Estimated)
*   **Infrastructure (Bridge & Roads):** $350M–$450M. This includes specialized engineering for seismic-resistant bridge design.
*   **Urban Housing & Commercial Restoration:** $300M–$500M. Focus on upgrading seismic compliance (codes) in rebuilt structures.
*   **Humanitarian & Health Support:** $100M–$150M. Long-term medical services and community mental health programs.
*   **Administrative & Planning:** $50M–$100M. Urban planning, mapping, and policy implementation.

### 3.2 Human Resource Requirements
*   **Engineering & Architecture:** ~200 professionals (Civil, Structural, Geotechnical).
*   **Construction Labor:** ~1,500 skilled tradespeople (Steel workers, concrete experts, electricians).
*   **Public Health & Social Services:** ~300 professionals (Medical, Psychological, Social Workers).

### 3.3 Material Requirements
*   **High-strength Reinforced Concrete & Steel:** Required for the bridge and high-rise reconstruction.
*   **Prefabricated Modular Units:** For interim residential and office solutions during the long-term reconstruction.
*   **Heavy Machinery:** Excavators, cranes, and debris processing equipment for the G5/G4 clearance phases.

## 4. Plan of Action

### 4.1 Phase I: Immediate Infrastructure & Stabilization (0–3 Months)
*   **Objective:** Restore basic connectivity and ensure public safety.
*   **Actions:**
    1.  Install temporary pontoon or Bailey bridge at the Concrete Bridge site.
    2.  Clear rubble from Hama Street to restore secondary access routes.
    3.  Finalize structural integrity audits for all G3 structures.
    4.  Establish secure, temporary housing for displaced residents in G1 zones.

### 4.2 Phase II: Structural Rehabilitation (3–18 Months)
*   **Objective:** Restore critical services and begin permanent building repair.
*   **Actions:**
    1.  Reconstruction of the permanent Concrete Bridge with base-isolation technology.
    2.  Debris removal and site preparation for the South Central Asaichi redevelopment.
    3.  Retrofitting and strengthening of G3-rated stone structures in North Asaichi.
    4.  Restoration of water, electricity, and telecommunications grid.

### 4.3 Phase III: Long-term Resilient Development (18+ Months)
*   **Objective:** Build back better, integrating seismic resilience.
*   **Actions:**
    1.  Full-scale urban planning, implementing modernized seismic construction standards.
    2.  Economic incentive programs for local business re-establishment.
    3.  Establishment of automated real-time seismic monitoring systems.

---

## 5. Recommendations
*   **Funding Policy:** Establish a dedicated "Regional Earthquake Recovery Fund" with matching state and local government contributions.
*   **Regulatory Reform:** Fast-track the adoption of updated building codes that require advanced seismic-damping technology for all new construction.
*   **Community Integration:** Involve local stakeholders in the urban planning process for the South Central Asaichi area to ensure the rebuilt environment meets community needs.
*   **Tax Incentives:** Implement temporary business tax relief for firms re-establishing operations in the Wajima region.
