# Emergency Alert: Post-Earthquake Safety Notice

**URGENT: Public Safety and Emergency Access Notice**

Following the recent seismic activity, a comprehensive structural assessment has been completed. The following alert is issued to ensure public safety and to facilitate ongoing rescue operations. Please strictly observe these directives:

### **🚫 NO-GO ZONES (EXTREME RISK - G5)**
*   **South Central Asaichi Street:** **TOTAL DEVASTATION.** Multiple buildings have collapsed; the ground is impassable and the area is currently uninhabitable. Do not attempt to enter.
*   **Concrete Bridge:** **TOTAL COLLAPSE.** This critical transit link is severed. Do not attempt to cross or enter the riverbank area surrounding the bridge ruins.

### **⚠️ RESTRICTED ACCESS (HIGH RISK - G4)**
*   **Hama Street:** **ROAD BLOCKED.** Severe structural failure has caused major debris accumulation. The street is cordoned off. Do not approach; wait for clearance from emergency personnel.

### **⚠️ CAUTIONARY ZONE (MODERATE RISK - G3)**
*   **North Asaichi Street:** Significant fractures have compromised structural integrity. If you are in this area, please exercise extreme caution and avoid remaining in or near stone-facade buildings.

---

### **✅ SAFE ZONES (G1)**
*   **Wajima Drama Memorial Hall & Central Nishikigawa Street:** These locations are stable and show no structural damage. Emergency services have designated these areas as primary support, triage, and command locations.

---

### **📢 News Update: Earthquake Impact Assessment**

**Wajima Region, Post-Earthquake Update:**
Authorities have confirmed a highly heterogeneous distribution of damage following the recent earthquake. While some sectors remain largely unaffected, others are facing catastrophic structural failures.

*   **Infrastructure Status:** The most critical concern is the destruction of the **Concrete Bridge**, which has severed a vital transit artery, significantly complicating rescue logistics.
*   **Rescue Priorities:** Emergency crews are currently focusing all efforts on **G5-rated zones (South Central Asaichi Street)**. Citizens are urged to stay clear of these zones to prevent accidents and allow rescue teams to navigate the debris.
*   **General Advice:** Avoid unnecessary travel to the Asaichi and Hama districts. Use the unaffected **Central Nishikigawa Street** area as a point of navigation and safety. 

**Help us help you: Keep main roads clear for emergency vehicle access. Do not attempt to bypass cordoned areas.**

*Stay tuned to local news channels for further updates. Your safety and cooperation are vital for the success of rescue operations.*
