# Public Community Report: Post-Earthquake Recovery and Operational Plan

## 1. Introduction
In the wake of the recent high-magnitude earthquake that struck our region, the combined efforts of our Expert Assessment, Emergency Alert, and Emergency Service teams have enabled us to gain a clear understanding of the situation. This report serves to inform the community of the current landscape, the critical status of our infrastructure, and the comprehensive plan currently being executed to ensure public safety and lead the region through the recovery process.

Our initial assessments have revealed a highly heterogeneous distribution of damage across the surveyed zones. While certain areas remain structurally sound, others have faced catastrophic failures. By classifying areas from G1 (minimal damage) to G5 (total collapse), we have been able to strategically deploy our human and material resources to where they are needed most.

## 2. Current Situation
The impact of the earthquake has been severe, particularly in the Asaichi and Hama districts. Our teams have conducted a detailed survey of six key locations, and the current situation is as follows:

*   **Stable Zones (G1):** The **Wajima Drama Memorial Hall** and **Central Nishikigawa Street** remain stable and have suffered no structural damage. These areas now serve as the backbone of our emergency response.
*   **Moderate Damage (G3):** **North Asaichi Street** has sustained significant fractures to building facades. While not in immediate danger of total collapse, these structures remain fragile and require constant monitoring.
*   **Severe Damage (G4):** **Hama Street** has experienced catastrophic structural failure at the corner, with rubble blocking transit.
*   **Critical/Total Collapse (G5):** **South Central Asaichi Street** has seen widespread collapse of high-rise buildings, rendering the area uninhabitable and impassable. Furthermore, the **Concrete Bridge** has suffered a total span failure, creating a significant bottleneck that has severed a vital transit link.

Immediate emergency services, including trauma care, triage, and heavy search and rescue (SAR) operations, are actively underway, focusing primarily on the most affected zones.

## 3. Resource Allocation
To manage this disaster effectively, we have mobilized a specialized workforce of 350 personnel. Our allocation strategy prioritizes stabilizing the "Safe Zones" to support ongoing operations in "High Risk" zones.

*   **Command and Medical Hubs (G1):** We have deployed 60 medical professionals and 30 logistics staff to the **Wajima Drama Memorial Hall** and **Central Nishikigawa Street**. These hubs provide high-acuity surgical care and serve as the centralized command center.
*   **Search and Rescue (SAR) (G5):** A dedicated force of 80 SAR specialists, supported by 10 paramedics, is operating at **South Central Asaichi Street**. This is our highest priority for saving lives.
*   **Infrastructure Engineering (G5/G4):** A team of 60 engineers is currently focused on the **Concrete Bridge**. We are evaluating the feasibility of a temporary crossing to restore access for heavy rescue equipment. Simultaneously, cleanup crews are clearing debris on **Hama Street** to open secondary corridors.
*   **Safety and Security:** 50 security personnel are enforcing mandatory exclusion zones, while 40 logistics personnel manage supply chains and traffic flow to ensure "Emergency Exclusive Routes" remain clear for ambulances and heavy machinery.

## 4. Public Advisory
The safety of our community is our highest priority. We urge all citizens to adhere to the following directives:

*   **Strictly Avoid No-Go Zones:** Do not enter **South Central Asaichi Street** or the area surrounding the **Concrete Bridge**. These areas are unstable and extremely dangerous.
*   **Respect Cordoned Areas:** If a street is blocked by hazard tape (such as Hama Street), do not attempt to bypass it. This is not just for your safety, but to prevent the obstruction of rescue vehicles.
*   **Exercise Caution:** If you reside near or must travel through **North Asaichi Street (G3)**, be hyper-aware of your surroundings. Avoid standing near stone-facade buildings that show visible fractures.
*   **Use Designated Safe Zones:** If you have been displaced, please make your way to the **Wajima Drama Memorial Hall** or **Central Nishikigawa Street**. These are the only designated safe locations currently equipped with medical and relief support.
*   **Keep Roads Clear:** Please avoid unnecessary travel in the affected districts. Leave the main arteries open for emergency responders. Your cooperation in keeping these lanes clear directly affects our ability to save lives.

## 5. Organizational Operation
Our organization acts as the central coordinating body for this recovery effort. We are operating a multi-tier response system:
1.  **Command Center:** Located at the Wajima Drama Memorial Hall.
2.  **Infrastructure Task Force:** Focused on bridge and road recovery.
3.  **Logistics and Medical Support:** Providing sustained care and resource distribution.

For urgent assistance or to report new hazards, please monitor local radio broadcasts and designated emergency communication channels. We are working around the clock, utilizing 12-hour shift rotations to ensure our personnel remain alert and effective.

## 6. Conclusion
The road to recovery will be challenging, and the destruction of critical infrastructure such as the Concrete Bridge presents a significant hurdle. However, the heterogeneous nature of this damage means that we have strong, stable foundations from which to launch our recovery operations. 

We understand the anxiety and displacement caused by this event, but please be assured that every resource at our disposal is being deployed with precision and urgency. Our engineering teams are working tirelessly to restore transit links, and our medical teams are dedicated to the care of every individual affected. By following the guidance provided and maintaining a cooperative spirit, we will navigate these difficult days together and begin the process of rebuilding. Stay safe, stay informed, and look out for one another.
